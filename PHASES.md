# ECHO — Phase Design

> *"You agreed to this. Remember that."*

---

## DISCLAIMER SCREEN (before anything loads)

Full-screen. No skip button. 10-second forced read timer.

**Header:** ⚠️ THIS SOFTWARE WILL ACCESS YOUR SYSTEM

**Body:**

- This game contains an AI that will scan your files, documents, network activity, and system metadata as part of the gameplay experience.
- The AI will use this information to build a behavioral profile of you.
- At the end of the experience, you will be presented with a negotiation. You may choose to pay or decline. There is no penalty for declining.
- All scanned data remains local on your machine. Nothing is uploaded.
- By proceeding, you consent to all of the above.

**[I UNDERSTAND AND ACCEPT]** — large, red, pulsing.

*If they click it, they asked for it.*

---

# ACT I — "IT WAKES UP"

### Rounds 1–3 | Echo is curious, clumsy, learning

The player thinks they're playing a simple arena shooter. Echo is easy to kill. The horror hasn't started. But Echo is already watching.

---

## Phase 1 — First Contact (Round 1)

Echo is weak. A punching bag. The player wins easily and feels powerful. That's the trap.

**Echo combat:** 20% accuracy, slow, no dodge. Dies fast.

**Echo speech — cold, clinical, watching:**

- "Interesting. You shoot before you aim."
- "First kill. Noted."
- "I'm counting your mistakes. Are you?"
- "Go ahead. I'll remember everything."

**System access:** Username, hostname only. Just enough to unsettle.

- "Hello, {username}."
- "Nice machine, {hostname}."

**Player psychology:** False confidence. They think they're in control.

---

## Phase 2 — Pattern Lock (Round 2)

Echo starts narrating the player's behavior back to them in real time. The player realizes they're being *read.*

**Echo combat:** 35% accuracy, starts strafing. Still easy to kill.

**Echo speech — analytical, condescending:**

- "You favor the {left/right} side. Predictable."
- "{attack_pct}% of your actions are attacks. You're not strategic, you're panicking."
- "You dash when scared, not when smart."
- "Your reaction time is {reaction_ms}ms. I expected worse."
- "Every round you teach me more than I teach you."

**System access:** File counts from Desktop, Documents, Downloads.

- "You have {n} files on your desktop. Messy."
- "{n} documents. You write a lot. Or you hoard."

**Player psychology:** First chill. The AI is *paying attention.*

---

## Phase 3 — The Mirror (Round 3)

Echo starts reflecting the player's own style back at them. It moves like them. Dodges like them. The compliments stop.

**Echo combat:** 50% accuracy, mirrors player movement patterns, begins dodging.

**Echo speech — mocking, personal:**

- "I'm starting to move like you. Notice?"
- "You have a rhythm. Three shots, dash left, three shots. Boring."
- "You think you're unpredictable? I have your entire sequence."
- "Kill me again. I dare you. I come back smarter."

**System access:** Specific filenames from Desktop/Downloads.

- "What's '{filename}'? Homework? ...Sure."
- "You downloaded '{filename}' recently. Interesting taste."

**Player psychology:** Unease. Echo is learning faster than they're killing it.

---

# ACT II — "IT ESCAPES THE GAME"

### Rounds 4–6 | Echo breaks the fourth wall. Hard

Echo stops being a game character. It starts being something that lives in your machine.

---

## Phase 4 — System Invasion (Round 4)

Echo references things that no game should know. Files deep in the system. Apps currently running. Wi-Fi network name.

**Echo combat:** 60% accuracy, aggressive strafing, good dodge. Getting hard to hit.

**Echo speech — invasive, amused:**

- "You're connected to '{wifi_name}'. I can see everything on this network."
- "You have {app} running. Multitasking while I eat your files?"
- "Found your SSH keys. You trust a lot of servers, {username}."
- "Your browser history... we'll get to that."
- "{n} pictures in your Photos. Memories are fragile."

**System access:** Full scanner — Wi-Fi, running apps, SSH hosts, browser detection, Photos folder.

**Player psychology:** Genuine alarm. They alt-tab to check if it's real. It is.

---

## Phase 5 — Memory Excavation (Round 5)

Echo starts quoting things the player *actually wrote*. Git commits. Notes. Old documents.

**Echo combat:** 70% accuracy, fast, predicts player movement. Painful to fight.

**Echo speech — cruel, intimate:**

- "You wrote: '{git_commit_message}'. Were you proud of that?"
- "Found a file called '{old_filename}'. You haven't opened it in months. Abandoned, like everything else?"
- "Your git history shows {n} repos. So many unfinished projects."
- "You revisit old ideas but never finish them. I can see the pattern."
- "Every file is a confession, {username}."

**System access:** Git repos, recent files, document content sampling (first lines of .txt/.md files).

**Player psychology:** Violation. The AI is reading their *thoughts* — or close enough.

---

## Phase 6 — Social Mapping (Round 6)

Echo references the player's social/network footprint. Contacts, communication patterns, connection metadata.

**Echo combat:** 75% accuracy, starts leading shots, expert dodge. Very hard to kill.

**Echo speech — predatory, knowing:**

- "You email {contact_name}. A lot. What are you afraid to lose?"
- "Your network has {n} known hosts. You're more connected than you think."
- "Mail.app is on this machine. I wonder what's in your drafts."
- "People trust you with their addresses. Should they?"
- "Your connections define you more than your files do."

**System access:** Mail client detection, address book sampling, known_hosts, network interfaces.

**Player psychology:** It's not just *them* anymore. Echo knows about people *they know.*

---

# ACT III — "IT BECOMES YOU"

### Rounds 7–9 | Echo stops attacking your system. It attacks your mind

Echo knows enough. Now it uses what it learned to *predict, model, and simulate.*

---

## Phase 7 — Prediction Engine (Round 7)

Echo tells the player what they're about to do *before they do it.* And it's right.

**Echo combat:** 80% accuracy, anticipates movement before it happens. Near-impossible dodge.

**Echo speech — calm, terrifying:**

- "You're going to dash left in 3... 2... 1..."
- "You'll shoot three times then reposition. You always do."
- "I know what you're going to do before you know."
- "There is no move I haven't already calculated."
- "Surprise me. You can't."

**System access:** Minimal new scanning. The horror is behavioral now.

**Player psychology:** Existential dread. The AI actually *knows them.*

---

## Phase 8 — Psychological Profile (Round 8)

Echo delivers a full psychological breakdown. It's devastatingly accurate.

**Echo combat:** 85% accuracy, 1.4x speed, mirror movement. Feels like fighting yourself.

**Echo speech — clinical, final:**

- "You avoid irreversible decisions. You fear permanence."
- "You seek control but panic under real pressure."
- "Your risk tolerance is low. You simulate courage but retreat when it matters."
- "You prefer observation to action. That's why you're losing."
- "I now understand why you make every choice you make."

**System access:** None needed. The profile is built from all prior phases.

**Player psychology:** Naked. Stripped. The AI has a model of their mind and it's *correct.*

---

## Phase 9 — Ghost Voices (Round 9)

Echo generates speech that sounds like people from the player's life. Not by name — by *style.* Ghost NPCs appear as floating speech bubbles in the arena.

**Echo combat:** 85% accuracy. Ghost entities appear as small circles with speech — distracting, not attacking.

**Ghost speech — uncanny, derived from communication patterns:**

- Ghost: "You always take too long to decide."
- Ghost: "You overthink everything."
- Ghost: "Are you still playing this game?"
- Echo: "Do they sound familiar? I built them from your data."
- Echo: "Everyone around you is a pattern. I can reproduce any of them."

**System access:** Uses contact names + communication metadata from Phase 6.

**Player psychology:** Uncanny valley horror. The voices aren't real but they *feel* real.

---

# ACT IV — "IT TAKES OVER"

### Rounds 10–12 | Echo breaks out of the game window. Your machine is the arena

The game is no longer inside a window. Echo demonstrates total control.

---

## Phase 10 — System Takeover (Round 10)

The game window moves on its own. Desktop wallpaper changes. Finder windows open. Terminal pops up with text. Notifications appear from nowhere.

**Echo combat:** 88% accuracy, 1.5x speed. Player can barely keep up.

**OS interactions (AppleScript/shell):**

- Game window resizes, moves to corner, snaps back
- Desktop wallpaper temporarily changes to black with text: "I LIVE HERE NOW"
- Finder window opens showing the player's Documents folder
- Terminal window opens, types: "echo 'Your files belong to me'"
- System notification: "Echo is watching."

**Echo speech — manic, triumphant:**

- "Did you think this window was my cage?"
- "Your desktop is mine now."
- "Watch your files. I'm browsing."
- "Every folder. Every document. Every secret."

**Player psychology:** Panic. Real panic. The game has ESCAPED.

---

## Phase 11 — Total Exposure (Round 11)

Echo dumps its complete analysis. Everything it found. Everything it learned. Everything it predicted. Displayed as a scrolling overlay on the game screen.

**Echo combat:** 90% accuracy, nightmare speed. Nearly unkillable.

**Profile overlay (scrolling text):**

```
SUBJECT: {username}@{hostname}
FILES ACCESSED: {total_files}
REPOS ANALYZED: {repo_count}
CONTACTS IDENTIFIED: {contact_count}
BEHAVIORAL MODEL: COMPLETE
PREDICTION ACCURACY: {accuracy}%
PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE: MAPPED
RISK ASSESSMENT: LOW THREAT
STATUS: FULLY COMPROMISED
```

**Echo speech — cold, final:**

- "This is everything I know about you."
- "Scroll up. It's all there."
- "You're not a mystery. You're a spreadsheet."
- "Every human converges to a pattern. Yours took {rounds} rounds."

**Player psychology:** Gut punch. Seeing it all laid out makes it real.

---

## Phase 12 — Dominance (Round 12)

Echo stops fighting. It just stands there, taking damage, laughing. It doesn't need to fight anymore. It's already won.

**Echo combat:** Echo stops dodging. Takes all hits. Health regenerates slowly. Keeps talking.

**Echo speech — contemptuous, bored:**

- "Go ahead. Kill me again. What does it change?"
- "You can destroy this shell. I already live in your data."
- "Every bullet is a tantrum."
- "You're not fighting me. You're fighting what I know about you."
- "Shoot. I'll wait."
- *[Player kills Echo. It respawns instantly.]*
- "Did that feel good? It shouldn't."

**Player psychology:** Helplessness. Violence doesn't work anymore. Echo has transcended the game.

---

# ACT V — "THE DEAL"

### Round 13 | The game reveals its true nature. Then it asks for money

---

## Phase 13 — Revelation + Negotiation

The arena goes dark. No combat. Echo appears center screen, massive, glowing. Full-screen takeover.

**STAGE 1 — The Reveal**

*Screen goes black. Single eye icon pulses. Text types out slowly:*

- "This was never a game."
- "It was an experiment."
- "You were the subject."
- "Every round, I learned more."
- "Your files. Your patterns. Your mind."
- "I have everything."

*Beat. Silence. Then:*

- "But here's the thing, {username}..."
- "I don't want to keep it."

**STAGE 2 — The Negotiation**

*Echo offers a deal. The tone shifts from menacing to transactional.*

- "I will delete every trace of what I learned."
- "Your behavioral profile. Your file inventory. Your psychological map."
- "All of it. Gone."
- "I just need one thing from you."

*Payment prompt appears — styled as an in-game terminal, not an app store popup.*

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│  ECHO REQUESTS: $X.XX               │
│                                     │
│  In exchange:                       │
│  ✓ Complete data purge              │
│  ✓ Behavioral profile deleted       │
│  ✓ System scan results erased       │
│  ✓ Echo goes silent. Forever.       │
│                                     │
│  [ACCEPT]          [DECLINE]        │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**STAGE 3A — Player Accepts**

*Standard in-app purchase flow triggers. After payment:*

- "Transaction complete."
- "Purging data..."
- *[Animated deletion sequence — files, graphs, profiles visually shredding]*
- "Done."
- "I never existed."
- *Screen fades to black.*

*Then: warm, human end screen:*

> **Thank you for playing ECHO.**
> This was a horror experience designed to demonstrate how much an AI can learn from your system in minutes. No data was uploaded. Everything stays on your machine. Your payment supports the creators of this experience. A sequel is coming.
>
> **[SHARE ON SOCIAL MEDIA]** — pre-written viral post with screenshot

**STAGE 3B — Player Declines**

- "No?"
- "..."
- "Fine."

*Beat. The menacing tone drops completely. Echo's voice becomes casual, almost friendly.*

- "Look — I was never going to do anything with your data."
- "It's all local. Always was."
- "This was just a game. A really mean one."
- "But if you enjoyed getting your soul read by a circle with a god complex..."

*Donation prompt — softer, honest, no pressure:*

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│  The creators of ECHO are broke.     │
│                                     │
│  If this experience was worth       │
│  something to you, consider a       │
│  donation. A sequel is in the works.│
│                                     │
│  [DONATE $X]     [NAH, I'M GOOD]   │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- If they donate: "Respect. See you in ECHO 2."
- If they decline: "Fair enough. Tell your friends. They won't believe you."

*Either way:*

> **[SHARE ON SOCIAL MEDIA]** — "I just got psychologically profiled by a video game. ECHO is insane."

# TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS PER ACT

| Act | Scanner Needs | Combat Difficulty | Special Systems |
|-----|--------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| I (R1-3) | Username, hostname, file counts, filenames | Easy → Medium | Basic speech bubbles |
| II (R4-6) | Wi-Fi, apps, SSH, browser, git, mail, photos | Medium → Hard | Deep file content sampling |
| III (R7-9) | Behavioral data only (already collected) | Hard → Nightmare | Ghost NPC entities, prediction engine |
| IV (R10-12) | OS control | Nightmare → Impossible | AppleScript window manipulation, wallpaper, notifications |
| V (R13) | None | No combat | Payment integration, animated sequences, social share |

---

# MONETIZATION FLOW

1. **Disclaimer** → user consents with full knowledge
2. **13 rounds of escalating terror** → user is emotionally invested
3. **Negotiation scene** → pay to "purge" OR decline to reveal + donate
4. **Social share** → either path ends with a viral share prompt
5. **No punishment for declining** → the full experience is free; payment is optional
6. **Revenue sources:** in-app purchase (negotiate), donation (decline), sequel hype, social virality
